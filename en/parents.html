How Mental Math Develops Children?

It's not just about the ability to count quickly. It's a powerful tool for the harmonious development of both hemispheres of a child's brain.

Concentration and Memory

Lessons teach the child to focus on the task without being distracted by external factors. Abacus visualization develops photographic memory.

School Success

Development of logic and thinking speed helps children master the school curriculum more quickly, not just in math, but in other subjects as well.

Self-Confidence

When a child realizes they can solve complex problems faster than adults, their self-esteem grows, which carries over to all areas of life.

Creative Potential

Working with mental images activates the right hemisphere, allowing the child to find creative solutions to any problems.
